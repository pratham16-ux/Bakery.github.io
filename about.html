Who We Are

A Bakery Built on
Flour, Water & Love

Levain & Co was born in a tiny kitchen in South London in 2003. Founder Isabelle Moreau had spent five years apprenticing under the legendary Michel Dumont in Lyon, and came home with one clear ambition: to bring genuine French artisan baking to her community.

Starting with just a home oven, a single starter culture (now over 20 years old), and a handwritten notebook of recipes, Isabelle began selling loaves from her front window on Saturday mornings. Word spread quickly. By 2005 she had signed a lease on Mill Lane. By 2010 she'd won her first Great Taste Award. By 2020 she had a team of twelve bakers and a waiting list for Saturday bread.

We still use the same starter. We still use the same notebook. We haven't changed anything about the way we bake — because it didn't need changing.

Milestones

Twenty Years in the Making

2003
The First Loaf
Isabelle returns from Lyon and bakes her first sourdough in a repurposed oven. Sells out in two hours from her front door.
2005
Mill Lane Opens
Our first proper bakery space. A small shopfront with a 3-deck stone oven and exactly four bar stools.
2010
First Great Taste Award ⭐
Our Country Sourdough wins a 2-star Great Taste Award — the first of seven we've received over the years.
2015
Baking School Launches
Weekend sourdough workshops begin. They sell out within minutes of opening and remain booked six months ahead.
2020
Community Kitchen Project
During lockdown, we baked and delivered over 2,000 loaves free-of-charge to NHS workers and elderly neighbours.
2023
20 Years of Baking 🎉
We celebrated two decades with a sold-out anniversary dinner, a limited-edition cookbook, and the same starter we began with.

What We Stand For

Our Values

🌾
Honest Ingredients

We use the best ingredients we can source — local where possible, organic where it matters, and nothing you can't pronounce.

⏳
Time is an Ingredient

We don't rush. Long fermentation, slow proving, careful baking — patience is what makes the difference between bread and great bread.

🤝
Community First

We're a neighbourhood bakery. We give day-old bread to food banks, support local schools, and try to genuinely earn the community's trust each day.

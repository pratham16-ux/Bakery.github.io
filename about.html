Who We Are

Born in a Tiny Kitchen,
Beloved by a City

Stackly Bakery started in 2003 when our founder, James Stackly, converted his Peckham kitchen into a weekend baking operation. A single sourdough recipe. A borrowed proving basket. And a determination to bake bread the honest way.

Within a year the neighbours were knocking on the door. Within three, we'd outgrown two kitchens. Today we operate from our 4,000 sq ft mill-bakery in Bermondsey — but the ethos has never changed: real ingredients, real time, real bread.

We believe bread is the most honest food there is. You cannot fake a great loaf — the ingredients, the time, and the baker's hands will always tell the truth.
— James Stackly, Founder

Our Journey

Twenty Years of Craft

2003
The Beginning
James bakes his first sourdough in his Peckham kitchen. Sells 12 loaves to neighbours. The phone number spreads by word of mouth.
2005
First Premises
We lease our first small unit on Bermondsey Street. Sarah joins as Head Pastry Chef, bringing her Parisian patisserie training. The croissant programme begins.
2009
The Great Expansion
A feature in The Guardian propels us to national attention. We hire our first apprentices and partner with three local wheat farms. Output triples in eight months.
2014
Mill-Bakery Opens
We install our own stone mill, allowing us to grind heritage grains on-site. The flavour change is instant and profound. The Guardian calls it "a watershed moment for British baking."
2018
School of Bread
We launch our baking classes, hosting over 2,000 students a year. Our YouTube channel reaches one million subscribers with a sourdough tutorial series.
2023
Twenty Years & Going Strong
We celebrate two decades with a limited 20th Anniversary Sourdough. All 500 loaves sell in 19 minutes. We receive the Best Artisan Bakery award for the fifth consecutive year.
2026
Today
We bake 480 loaves and 600+ pastries every single day. We still use the same starter culture James cultivated in 2003. The bread still tastes like it always did — because we've never changed how we make it.

How We Bake

The Art of Real Bread

Five deliberate steps. No shortcuts. No compromises. This is how every loaf has been made since 2003.

🌾
Mill
Heritage grains stone-milled in-house within 48 hours of baking for maximum flavour
💧
Mix
Flour, water, salt and live starter — combined by hand in precise ratios, every morning
⏰
Ferment
48–72 hours cold fermentation. Patience is our secret ingredient
🙌
Shape
Each loaf hand-shaped and scored with our signature cuts — the baker's signature on every piece
🔥
Bake
Wood-fired deck oven at 260°C — a perfect crust in under 40 minutes
